How to Find Distribution

The most basic thing to do:

  1. Look up the film on www.imdb.com.
  2. When you pull up the film as a full page, look in the left hand column. Click on "Company credits."
  3. If it's a major distributor (one you've heard of), write this down - you're done for now. If you're not sure, click on the distributor to see if it has any recent films listed. If there's nothing from the past fifty years, it's probably no good. If there are recent films listed, go ahead and write it down.

If you haven't heard of the company/there isn't one in the U.S.:

Try the websites below (Swank, Kino, Cowboy). Terrific if you find it at one of these - write it down and you're done for now.

Swank: www.swank.com

  1. Click on College Campuses.
  2. Click on Movie Index.
  3. Click on "Click Here" to find a specific title, or browse.
  4. Type in the most important word in the film (spelling counts!) and if nothing pulls up try other single random words.

Kino: www.kino.com

  1. Click on Theatrical/Non-theatrical.
  2. Look up the title in both the Theatrical and Non-Theatrical sections.

Cowboy: www.cowboypictures.com

  1. Browse from the upper left hand corner.

Criterion: www.criterionpicusa.com

  1. Enter.
  2. Click on the "Search Our Database" tab.

Still can't find it?

Look it up on www.google.com. Click on any links to film festivals. Find contact names, telephone numbers, email addresses, or whatever else I might be able to use to reach someone involved in organizing the festival. Be sure to include this information in the "Distribution" for each film in your series proposal.

Hope this helps! Email me ASAP if you need help with this stuff.

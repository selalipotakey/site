Wednesdays

June 25      8:00
Frenzy
Alfred Hitchcock, 1972         116m

Hitchcock crafted some of his finest films around individuals mistakenly pursued by the coppers, from Young and Innocent to Saboteur to North by Northwest. In Frenzy, Richard Blaney is framed for murder after having unwittingly been taken in by the psychopathic "Necktie Murderer" Robert Rusk. While Blaney flees for his very life, Chief Inspector Oxford of Scotland Yard pursues him relentlessly, but only when not suffering the culinary abuses of his wife.

July 2         8:00
Strangers on a Train
Alfred Hitchcock, 1951         101m

Be careful what you wish for, and be careful who you share your wishes with. Tennis champion Guy Haines (Farley Granger) wishes to divorce his promiscuous wife to remarry. On the train to his fiancée's home, he meets Bruno Anthony (Robert Walker), who wants his father dead. They agree to kill each other's obstacles to happiness, but Bruno doesn't get the joke. Soon after, Guy's wife is murdered, and Bruno's intrusions into Guy's life become ever more terrifying.  

July 9         8:00
The Birds
Alfred Hitchcock, 1963         119m

A film that Hitchcock himself proclaimed as one of his "scariest," The Birds has been making pet owners look at their animals in a slightly different way for 40 years. Socialite Melanie Daniels (Tippi Hedren) drives to the small coastal village of Bodega Bay in an innocent attempt to deliver a pair of love birds. But shortly after she arrives, the area birds begin exhibiting odd behavior. Soon after, nature takes hold and the birds begin savagely attacking local townspeople. Beware: after watching, you may never enter a phone booth again.

July 16       8:00
Knife in the Water
Roman Polanski, 1962         94m

The complex psychology between a successful bourgeois, his wife, and the young stranger they run into on holiday provide the tension and suspense in this unpredictable and atmospheric thriller from Polanski, at the height of his Polish cinema fame. The self-confidant Andrzej convinces a stranger to go sailing after almost hitting him with his car. On the boat, Andrzej relentlessly provokes him, despite warning signs that the stranger is not harmless.

July 23       8:00
Repulsion
Roman Polanski, 1965         104m

Though Polanski's first film in English, this creepy thriller sees some of its best moments in silence, as the camera voyeuristically follows Catherine Deneuve around her solitary London flat. As loneliness and sexual frustration set in, the young girl slowly, quietly goes insane.  Polanski, who later directed Rosemary's Baby, recreates her horrid nightmares with uneasy, surrealistic images, including some of the weirdest walls you'll ever see.

July 30       8:00
Cul-de-Sac
Roman Polanski, 1966         111m

A castle just off the English coast provides a suitable hiding place for two criminals on the run. But inside this castle, things are far from normal. The castle's inhabitants are not the type one would expect to find just off England.  George (Donald Pleasence) has a most unusual relationship with his French wife Teresa (Françoise Dorléac), and the weirdness spreads to the relationship between the two unwilling hosts and their criminal gatecrashers.

August 6    8:00
Juve vs. Fantômas
Louis Feuillade, 1913 64m

One chapter and one complete tale from director Feuillade's magnificent and ground-breaking "Fantômas" serial. The Sûreté, represented by the resourceful Inspector Juve, are out to stop the nefarious Fantômas, a thief with a penchant for disguise and a dangerous animus. Their meeting features not only the Holmes-and-Moriarty style mental duels, intrigues, and twists, but also include a touch of fantasy and magic. This was mostly shot on location in and around Paris.

August 13         8:00
Menilmontat
DiMitri Kirsanov, 1924          25m

A beautiful and moving film on the subject of love and violence in Paris, and how it affects two orphaned sisters.

The Blood of a Poet
Jean Cocteau, 1932         55m

This the first in Cocteau's experimental "Orphic Trilogy" follows a poet through a series of dream-like occurences wrought with surrealist imagery and symbolism. The poet starts sketching faces, but when one comes alive he launches into an exploration of the potential of film to obscure reality.

August 20         8:00
Eyes Without A Face
Georges Franju, 1959          88m

Far from prying eyes, in a chateau in the French woods, lives a surgeon (Pierre Brasseur), his mistress (Alida Valli) and his daughter. Due to an automobile accident with her father, the little girl has only eyes and no face under the blank mask she wears. Desperate to correct his wrong, the father sends his mistress to the countryside to procure face after face for the girl. All transplants have failed so far, leaving only faceless victims and increasing madness in their wake.

August 27         8:00
Day of Wrath
Carl Theodor Dreyer, 1943          110m

Dreyer often centered his narratives on religion, and often on the persecution and ostracism of individuals. Like his masterful The Passion of Joan of Arc, Day of Wrath concerns historical persecution by the church for witchcraft, this time in 17th Century Denmark. The accusations of trafficking with the Devil are first flung at an old woman, but after the death of the town pastor, his mother accuses the pastor's young wife of the same, and demands justice.


Thursdays

June 26      8:00
La Symphonie Pastorale
Jean Delannoy, 1946          110m

Author André Gide had suffered greatly both from the loss of his wife in 1938 and the French defeat in 1940, with France's internal re-organization in conflict with his humanist ideology. Gide's novel, La Symphonie Pastoral, seemed perfect for a France struggling to rationalize its wartime actions. A blind woman (Michèle Morgan) is taken in by a man of the church, entagling her in an unexpected and disturbing relationship with the minister, his son, and her eventual sight.

July 3         8:00
The Adventures of Robin Hood
Michael Curtiz, 1938   102m

Errol Flynn is Robin Hood, swinging from trees to rob the rich and give to the poor. While tangling with bad Prince John (Claude Rains) and Gisbourne (Basil Rathbone), he maintains his charm, wit, and bright smile for Maid Marian (Olivia de Havilland). Director Curtiz used the magnificent flair of Warner's at their swashbuckling height, shot in luminous Technicolor, and Erich Wolfgang Korngold provided the score, creating this most wonderful tale.

July 10       8:00
Diabolique
Henri-Georges Clouzot, 1955     88m

This mature drama follows the actions of the wife and mistress of a sadistic school headmaster as they plot his murder. They decide to drown him and dump his body in a pool, but when the body disappears and sightings of the headmaster are reported, the two women find themselves entangled in an impossible mystery far beyond simple murder. Clouzot's Diabolique is a masterful study of pure suspense that is guaranteed to have your heart pounding.

July 17       8:00
Dressed to Kill
Roy William Neill, 1946       72m

Holmes set the standard for detective fiction with his coldly rational reasoning powers despite being the creation of Sir Arthur Conan Doyle; country doctor, author of "The Lost World," and subscriber to theories of astral projection. Though a good Peircian semiotician may quarrel with the famous "deductive reasoning," the solution remains elementary to the great detective. In this installment of the cases of Holmes, he and Watson come across a series of random unrelated murders and must discover the sinister motive behind them!

July 24       8:00
The Sound Barrier
David Lean, 1952       111m

David Lean pays homage to the frontiersmen of British aerospace in the jet age, while exploring the psychology he would later develop in Lawrence of Arabia and Bridge on the River Kwai. The film focuses on a test pilot, his marriage to Susan Ridgefield (Ann Todd),the risky life of her brother Christopher and an aircraft firm headed by their father, John Ridgefield (Ralph Richardson). The stunning aerial photography and magisterial score accentuate the narrative balancing act of scientific progress versus the lives of those on the edge.

July 31       8:00
Glen or Glenda?
Ed Wood, 1953          67m

Ed Wood is heralded as the "world's worst filmmaker," and this film journies to the depths and heights of his almost lyrical incompetence.  Glen/Glenda is a troubled transvestite (Ed Wood) with a passion for angora accessories and a goal of surgical intervention.  The film has a distinctly hybrid character, combining a police investigation, hilarious melodramatic confrontations, demonic S&M dream sequences, and seemingly unmotivated inserts of stock footage.

August 7    8:00
Putney Swope
Robert Downey, Sr., 1969          84m

Beginning the fine tradition of Blazing Saddles and Trading Places, Putney Swope makes the racial switcheroo comedy possible. Its release in 1969 represented a true milestone: this was the sort of racial commentary Hollywood happily kept locked in the basement. A rich white corporate board plots to pass power from one cracker to another. But the honkeys miscalculate, resulting in the ascendancy of the board's token brother, Putney Swope. Unsatisfied with the company's lack of color, Swope re-invents Wall Street with a taste for what's hip.

August 14         8:00
Destiny
Fritz Lang, 1921          110m

Lang's Destiny (originally Der Müde Tod, and known in English also as The Weary Death) combines an existential plot with the creative film-making design that later would come to be called German Expressionism. Death has taken a young man to the other side. Desperate to save her love's life, his fiancée strikes a deal. She is transplanted into three different places and different times charged with saving one life in exchange for that of her fiancé.

August 21         8:00
Cops
Keaton & Cline, 1922          18m

The Paleface
Keaton & Cline, 1922          11m

Seven Chances
Buster Keaton, 1925         56m

In Cops, Buster is chased by a lot of police, and in The Paleface he finds himself paired with a victimized Native American tribe. Following these successes, Keaton made Seven Chances, the story of a down-on-his-luck bachelor who finds he will inherit a fortune, but only if he can find a wife by the end of the day.

August 28         8:00
World for Ransom
Robert Aldrich, 1954         82m

South Seas drifter Mike Callahan (Dan Duryea) came back from war to find his girlfriend, Frennessey, married to his friend Julian. Now, a decade later, Julian is working for Singapore's crime boss (Gene Lockhart). Frennessey seeks Callahan's aid in investigating her hustband by promising to come back to him. Callahan, unsure of his future but still in love, agrees. Unknown to him, the future of the world may rest on the points of this bitter love triangle.


Fridays
June 27      7:00, 9:15
Fallen Angels
Wong Kar-wai, 1995        96m

A hitman decides to give up his trade and leave his beautiful partner in the lurch because she set him up (though they've never met). Meanwhile, a young, mute ex-con who lives with his dutiful father plies a number of unusual trades in Hong Kong's night streets. Fallen Angels may feel like a more-polished remake of Wong's influential Chungking Express, but dig deeper and be surprised at the depth of expression he weaves around his themes of isolation and loneliness.

July 4         7:00, 9:15
Happy Together
Wong Kar-wai, 1997         96m

A young gay couple, Yiu-Fai and Po-Wing have their share of relationship troubles. Believing that starting anew in another country will solve their problems, they leave Hong Kong for Buenos Aires only to have difficulties lead to separation. Yiu-Fai works long hours in order to return to Hong Kong, but Po-Wing's subsequent return to his life complicates matters even further. Wong Kar-wai won a well-deserved Best Director award at Cannes for this film.

July 11       7:00, 9:30
Damn Yankees!
G. Abbott & S. Donen, 1958      111m

In an adaptation of the hit Broadway musical, senior citizen superfan Joe Boyd makes a pact with the devil. Joe gives his soul to become the greatest baseball player and help his favorite baseball team, the Washington Senators, win the pennant. When Joe becomes a youthful and talented player, a pennant win is within reach, but the distance from his beloved wife, attempted seductions, and the Devil's interference make winning a more complicated issue than originally expected.

July 18       7:00, 9:00
The Little Shop of Horrors
Roger Corman, 1960          70m

Perhaps the only movie where a talking plant upstages the human actors, this cult classic comedy stars Jonathan Haze as Seymour Krelboyne, a young, bumbling clerk who thinks that his job in the flower store will be a safe change of place. But Seymour produces a giant, talking, man-eating plant that needs human flesh to survive. At first Seymour donates his own blood to feed the plant, but it eventually convinces him to kill other people to feed its ferocious appetite.

July 25       7:00, 9:00
Dude, Where's My Car?
Danny Leiner, 2000    83m

After a night of debauchery, So-Cal stoners Jesse (Ashton Kutcher) and Chester (Seann William Scott) have forgotten where they parked their car. As they retrace their steps and the mysteries of the previous night multiply, the two encounter an enraged street gang, a group of alien fanatics, and their angry girlfriends. Follow these feckless losers in their hungover, perpetually-high daze and learn what its like to be the life of the party after the party is over.

August 1    7:00, 9:00
The Umbrellas of Cherbourg
Jacques Demy, 1964         90m

From the director of Lola and The Young Girls of Rochefort, this wonderful film cleverly pays homage to the institution of the Hollywood musical, while ironically skewering the pretensions of same. Catherine Deneuve stars as Geneviève, a young woman pursued by two men who both love her dearly. One is her husband, one the father of her child. Deneuve's performance here, at age 20, catapulted her into cinema and began a career that would span forty years.

August 8    7:00, 9:00
Duck Soup
Leo McCarey, 1933   70m

In this classic, Rufus T. Firefly (Groucho Marx), recently named dictator of the tiny country of Fredonia, immediately begins launching insults. Ambassador Trentino of Sylvania (Louis Calhern), a constant target of Firefly's wit, declares war over his hurt pride. In addition to the trademark gags, intrigue abounds, especially with peanut salesman, spy, and minister Chicolini (Chico Marx). What results is a magnificent depction of the absurdities of war.

August 15         7:00, 9:15
The Dangerous Lives of Altar Boys
Peter Care, 2002        104m

Oh, those Catholic school hijinks! Francis Doyle (Emile Hirsch) and Tim Sullivan (Kieran Culkin) are two teenage boys suffering through their humdrum parochial education in 1970's Georgia the only way they know how: by drawing a comic book and making life for Sister Assumpta (Jodie Foster) a living hell. Interspersed with original animation by comic book artist Todd McFarlane (Spawn), this is a funny, sincere take on what it is to be a rebellious teenager.

August 22         7:00, 9:00
Rancho Notorious
Fritz Lang, 1952          89m

Fritz Lang, director of Metropolis and Destiny, escaped Germany to come to Hollywood, where his films spanned several genres. Some of his most intriguing work are his Westerns. In this psychologically charged plains picture, Vern Haskall (Arthur Kennedy of The Lusty Men and Lawrence of Arabia) vociferously trails a bandit who robbed, raped and killed his fiancée. The only lead he has is a dying man's words. But what, or where, is "chuck-a-luck"?

August 29         7:00, 9:15
Belle de jour
Luis Buñuel, 1967       101m

Often considered Buñuel's masterpiece, Belle de jour fuses surreal fantasy with unsatisfying reality. Trapped in a chaste marriage with a husband she deeply loves in every way except sensually, French housewife Séverine (Catherine Deneuve) seeks out other ways to vent her sexual frustrations. Her panaceas grow from masochistic fantasies to part-time work at a local brothel under the pseudonym "Belle de jour." At first, Séverine's husband (Jean Sorel) hasn't a clue.


Saturdays

June 28      7:00, 9:15
Brother
Aleksei Balabanov, 1997    96m

Danila, unable to find a job admid the harsh capitalism of rural Russia, heads to St. Petersburg to see if his older brother can help. As a dispassionate and coldly capable military marksman, Danila joins his brother's established murder-for-hire business. But killing for country and killing for aggrandized petty thievery are not quite the same, and Danila finds life disorienting out of uniform and in the new, free, kleptocratic Russia .

July 5         7:00, 9:00
The House of Yes
Mark S. Waters, 1997         85m

So-called "Indie-queen" Parker Posey, teen "heart-throb" Freddie Prinze Jr., and 90210 "star" Tori Spelling combine their talents to bring Wendy MacLeod's darkly comedic play of romance, incest, do-it-yourself Kennedy assassinations, anti-insanity drugs, and love, to the screen. Generally insane and occasionally homicidal Jackie-O awaits the return of Marty, her twin brother/lover/gunshot victim for Thanksgiving dinner. But - oh dear - Marty is bringing his new fiancée.

July 12       7:00, 9:15
Tron
Steven Lisberger, 1982      96m

What do you get when you put huge 1980's computers together with a campy visionary style and state-of-the-art special effects? Tron.  Video arcade owner Kevin Flynn (Jeff Bridges) is convinced that his programs have been stolen by his boss. While hacking late one night, Flynn is pulled into the computer where he meets up with other video programmers.  Together, these heroes of the digital age must survive gladiatorial contests and find their way back to the real world.

July 19       7:00, 9:00
Run Lola Run
Tom Tykwer, 1998     80m

Tykwer's stylized film follows Lola (Franka Potente) on a twenty-minute dash through the streets of Berlin while she tries to somehow locate 20,000 DM to save her boyfriend Manni (Mortiz Bleibtreu) from a local gang. Set roughly in real time, Lola's twenty-minute journey is shown in three variations. Featuring a tub-thumping soundtrack, it demonstrates how small variations in everyday activity can change the larger trajectory of everything, and everyone, nearby.

July 26       7:00, 9:30
Safe
Todd Haynes, 1995   119m

While things seem to be going as they would normally, Carol White (Julianne Moore) starts to develop reactions to all sorts of things; everything in her San Fernando Valley housewife life from the hairdressers to the sofa. Her doctor and husband are no help, and Carol finds herself fleeing to be among her kind of weirdly reactant people, safe in an oddly appropriate spa. What's wrong with Carol White? What's the solution to her problem? What is that buzzing noise?

August 2    7:00, 10:00
The Last Temptation of Christ
Martin Scorsese, 1988        164m

This controverisal adaptation of Nikos Kazantzakis' novel is a portrait of a human and very Catholic Christ Ð one who grapples with temptation and is uncertain if he is truly God's son. Although much attention was paid to the film's depiction of Jesus' desire for a sexual relationship with Mary Magdalene, the re-telling of Judas' story is also noteable. In effect, Judas becomes the hero, and his betrayal of Jesus proves central and necessary to his Messianic mission.

August 9    7:00, 9:15
The Woman in White
Peter Godfrey, 1948          109m

Count Fosco (Sydney Greenstreet), urbane and debonair, has a subtle and cunning plan to destroy two young women, the beautiful but frail Laura and her friend and kindly relative, Marian. Their teacher, Hartright, discovers something of the plan in his visits, from Fosco's wife (Agnes Moorehead), and from a mysterious woman in white, but finds himself unable to stop the dastardly turn of events. From the 1860 novel by mystery novel pioneer Wilkie Collins.

August 16    7:00, 9:30
Frantic
Roman Polanski, 1988         120m

Convention-bound doctor Richard Walker (Harrison Ford) arrives in Paris with his wife. Soon after, his wife disappears. Walker, initially perplexed, descends into the dark side of Paris. To Walker, an American of good standing, this is unfamiliar ground indeed, and Polanski uses his mastery of the unsettling to maximize the feeling of alienation and steadily increases the tension through every suspenseful, frantic moment of the film.

August 23     7:00, 9:15
Blow-Up
Michelangelo Antonioni, 1966      111m

Image and reality combine in the swinging society of London's 1960s. Fashion- photographer Thomas (David Hemmings) doesn't care anything about his wild, swinging lifestyle, but the mystery of the blowup photo begins to consume him. In this film, his first English language feature, Antonioni matches psychology with his always-amazing cinematography. What results is an investigation of the image every way as fascinating and complex as Thomas'.

August 30      7:00, 9:00
Atomic Café
Loader & Rafferty, 1982      92m

"Duck and Cover" yourself for this hilarious send-up of America's atomic culture in all its blind fear and silly gimmickry. A collection of disturbingly hilarious film clips featuring everything from the government's stiffly knowledgeable experts to animated turtles, Atomic Café presents the mentality where the word atomic would sell a drink or start a panic. But not to worry: if you see an atomic bomb go off, just get in a gutter or cover yourself with a blanket: You'll be Okay!

Mondays - History of Animation

March 28 7:00
Early Animation
80m

From one of the first animated films, Stuart Blackton's Humorous Phases of Funny Faces (1905), to the adulterous beetle caught on camera in Starewicz's Revenge of a Kinematograph Cameraman, early animation was concentrated in a few master hands. Winsor McCay hand-drew 25,000 frames for his The Sinking of the Lusitania. This week looks at some of these individuals, namely McCay, Blackton, Cohl, and Porter, and features Frame by Frame, a documentary by Paul Burnford providing a brief overview of several animation techniques in film.

April 4 6:00
Germans
140m

Hardly a decade after the works of McCay and Cohl, several German animators continued to discover new realms of animation. Fischinger was famous for his innovative commercials, working hard to conceal his secrets. Walter Ruttmann explored the abstract purity possible in animation, and Lotte Reiniger produced the first feature-length animated film, in a rare silhouette technique. Fernand Leger's classic Ballet Mecanique, which pushed the boundaries of film, is included, as is L'Idee by Berthold Bartosch, who actually worked with Reiniger before moving to films of his own.

April 11 7:00
Introduced by Don Crafton, Chair of Studies in Film, Television, and Theatre, University of Notre Dame
Studio Era
130m

Animation radically changed with the advent of studio-based production, becoming an entire industry with a direct and profitable appeal to a mass audience. Walt Disney, the Fleischer brothers, William Hanna, and Chuck Jones are just a few of the incomparable personalities of the era. Don't miss the all-time favorites The Old Mill and Cat Concerto, along with the first "synchronized"-sound animated film, Steamboat Willy, award-winning Flowers and Trees, and classic characters including Popeye, Road Runner, and Felix the Cat.

April 18 7:00
What's Opera, Doc?
Chuck Jones, 1957 7m
Fantasia
Walt Disney, 1940 120m

A true classic, Fantasia takes the fusion of music and animation in Steamboat Willy and Bugs Bunny's mock-operatic roles to a whole new level. The Philadelphia Orchestra is conducted by Leopold Stokowski, in a rare and fantastic collaboration. A visual and aural feast, Fantasia is a fantastic film for families and people of all ages and also the prime example of an animated film that both broke new ground and appealed to audiences everywhere.

April 25 7:00
Lye and McLaren
150m

Len Lye and Norman McLaren are two giants of direct animation, a technique which involves drawing directly on the film strip itself, rather than with traditional cells. While deeply invested with the notion of the material of film, both Lye and McLaren thoroughly explored the relation and capacity of their drawings to musical accompaniment. Lye is relatively obscure to many people, though powerful in a very personal way. For those of you from Canada, you very well may have grown up watching cartoons from the National Film Board, under which McLaren produced much of his work. Short works by Brakhage, Jordan, and Breer are also featured.

May 2 7:00
Hubley
100m

This week is a tribute to the revolutionary animation of the Hubleys. Their films were conceptually complicated and stylistically radical. While still appealing to a mass audience, they sought to distance themselves from the assembly-line standards of titans such as Disney. This week also looks at the unique work of Alexeieff and his "pin animation," a technique which has rarely been employed, despite its provocative results and capabilities. He constructed his own pinboards, one containing over 1 million pins! Vera Neubauer and Robert Cannon, also featured, had a drive in many ways similar to the Hubleys, though still distinct and worth a reception of their own.

May 9 7:00
Introduced by Tom Gunning, Committee on Cinema and Media Studies
Quay Brothers and Janie Geiser
120m

The brothers Quay are strong devotees to the provocative surrealism of Jan Svankmajer (one of the night's screenings is their Cabinet of Jan Svankmajer). The Quays maintain a hands-on approach to their work, rather than merely conceptualizing and having others do the stop animation. Janie Geiser is renowned for her filmmaking, illustration, and puppetry. While she only started filming in 1990, her work is influential and known for its sense of mystery and self-contained worlds. These individuals have expanded the bounds of animation.

May 16 7:00
Stop Animation?
140m

These films explore the relation of animation to reality. Headlining tonight is Anders Rønnow-Klarlund's Strings, a feature-length tale of a king's son who sets out on a journey to avenge the death of his father. In Janice Findley's A Nermish Gothic, a young woman is chased around by a glowing cone until she retaliates with her giant hairnet. Intrigued? Also see her film Beyond Kabuki and Standish Lawder's Runaway, a reflection on animation, called "a modern Ballet Mecanique" by Herman Weinberg. George Griffin's Trickfilm 3 is a combination of live action and animation that has a long history, all the way back to the Fleischer Out of the Inkwell, where Betty Boop was born.

May 23 7:00
Introduced by Malynne Sternstein, Department of Slavic Languages and Literatures
Slavic Animation
170m

Some of the most fascinating animation of all time is from Eastern Europe, by artists like Jan Svankmajer and Jiri Trnka. Svankmajer is featured in this screening, with his feature-length adaptation of Alice in Wonderland, complete with tack-jam and characteristically disturbing creatures. There is also one of his short films—Tma svetlo tma (Darkness Light Darkness), fascinating in itself and as an allegory of the constraints of totalitarianism. Svankmajer is here accompanied by Trnka and Boroczyk, provocative and politically outspoken in their works.

May 30 7:00
The New Animation
130m

The ground-breaking Toy Story, from Pixar Studios, is featured tonight, accompanied by several short works: Bill Plympton's 25 Ways to Quit Smoking, Nirvan Mullick's The Box Man, Paul Fierlinger's A Room Nearby, the Spiridellis Team's popular This Land, and Martha Colburn's Evil of Dracula and There's a Pervert in our Pool! This program is explores the new frontiers of animation, displaying its great potential. The advent of the Internet has changed production and exhibition, providing easy access to a whole host of short films from independent artists and larger companies, such as Britain's Aardman Animation (famous for Wallace and Gromit and more).

Tuesdays - Friends & Lovers

March 29 7:00
Bonnie and Clyde
Arthur Penn, 1967 111m

Bonnie Parker (Faye Dunaway) first sees Clyde Barrow (Warren Beatty) from her bedroom window as he tries to steal her family's car. Rather than reporting him to the authorities, she has the good sense to hit the road with him after a little necking in the back seat. They become robbers on a whim, although in the rural south of depression-era America, this isn't a very lucrative trade. Wanted signs multiply as their resources dwindle and their gang grows. Clyde's brother Buck (Gene Hackman in his prime) and his high-strung wife cause trouble, but it is CW who does them in as it becomes apparent that their fame will overtake them. The period soundtrack is by banjo greats, Flatt and Scruggs. 16 mm

April 5 6:00
Sid and Nancy
Alex Cox, 1986 111m

Sid Vicious (Gary Oldman), bass player and ne'er-do-well of the notorious punk band, the Sex Pistols, is a drug-addicted squatter who doesn't know how to play bass. He and his band are the vanguard of a new scene that believes in nothing: no jobs, no style, no respect, no future. He meets an American drug-addicted squatter named Nancy Spungen (Chloe Webb), and as the band blows up, Sid and Nancy drink, shoot heroin, fight and screw until the bitter end. This movie gives a lovingly squalid portrait of an underestimated subculture. 35mm

April 12 7:45
Fanny och Alexander
Ingmar Bergman, 1982 188m

In Sweden, at the beginning of the twentieth century, two children undergo many profound and difficult transformations. We follow watchful Alexander (Bertil Guve) and his little sister Fanny (Pernilla Allwin) through the lavish Christmas celebrations of their enormous theatrical family, then through the death of their father and their mother's remarriage to a comically, righteously evil Bishop, and their triumphant, supernatural escape. Bergman had said that this film was his most autobiographical (no word on whether Ismael was real, however). By turns hilarious, spooky and moving, this is one of the most watchable Bergman films. 35mm

April 19 7:00
Thelma and Louise
Ridley Scott, 1991 129m

This is the definitive women's road-trip adventure film, replete with sexual awakening and massive explosions. Unhappy at work and pissed at her boyfriend, Louise (Susan Sarandon) dreams of taking off. Her friend Thelma (Geena Davis) suffers an even more dire unhappiness, stuck at home with a lug of a husband. A cat-and-mouse game with the FBI begins after Louise shoots a man who is sexually assaulting Thelma. Instead of turning themselves in, the women drive towards the Mexican border, ardently pursued by Hal (Harvey Keitel) and a growing cloud of police. As the women realize they can't go home again, their bond to each other becomes insoluble. 35mm

April 26 7:00
Manny and Lo
Lisa Krueger, 1996 88m

Honored as a "Sundance Fellow", director Krueger workshopped this film with the usual cast of Utah luminaries in 1994. In this, her first feature-length project, two sisters run away from their respective foster homes to hit the road together. Manny, born Amanda (Scarlett Johansson), is a serious-minded 11 year-old. Laurel, or Lo (Aleksa Palladino) is a 16 year-old whose dream of becoming a flight attendant may be thwarted by her ever-more-apparent pregnancy. At Manny's insistence, Lo grudgingly kidnaps Elaine (Mary Kay Place), the clerk at a baby-goods store. Elaine turns out to be trouble for Lo, but Manny finds in her a sympathetic maternal figure. 35mm

May 3 7:00
Jules et Jim
François Truffaut, 1962 105m

Two best friends, a German (Jules, Oskar Werner) and a Frenchman (Jim, Henri Serre) go to Greece to find a statue of a beautiful woman, but on their way back, they find the real thing. Catherine (Jeanne Moreau) is the wedge between them and the tie that binds them, even as the First World War begins and the men are on opposite sides of the line. She is the original wacky girlfriend character, before it became an unavoidable cliché. This film, Truffaut's second best (after The 400 Blows), and a French New Wave classic, had an immeasurable impact on later filmmakers (the screenwriter of Bonnie and Clyde named his first daughter Catherine). 35mm

May 10 7:00
Harold and Maude
Hal Ashby, 1971 91m

Harold (Bud Cort, in the role that made him an icon but ruined his career) is a rich, death-obsessed, teenage misanthrope, whose out-to-lunch mother constantly sets him up on dates (that he ruthlessly sabotages). His outlook brightens when he meets the madcap Maude (Ruth Gordon), a vivacious, elderly, pot-smoking Holocaust survivor, who, like him, attends funerals recreationally. The two wreak havoc on the highway, take on the military, and find meaning together - until Maude's eightieth birthday. The sweetest love story to begin with a funeral and end with a car crash features a goofy soundtrack by Yusuf Islam (formerly Cat Stevens, formerly Steven Katz). 35mm

May 17 7:00
Benny & Joon
Jeremiah Chechik, 1993 98m

Benny (Aidan Quinn) has always taken care of his unbalanced little sister Juniper (Mary Stuart Masterson). His attempts to perform this task have ended in disaster, until, one night in a game of poker, Benny acquires Sam (the one and only Johnny Depp), his friend's vagrant cousin. Sam is a charming, illiterate Buster Keaton fanatic with unorthodox housekeeping methods, and Joon quickly warms to him. When they inevitably fall in love, Benny tries to stand in their way, but the couple overcomes this and other obstacles with touched (and touching) methods. The supporting cast includes indie favorites Oliver Platt, Julianne Moore, and William H. Macy. 35mm

May 24 7:00
Fando y Lis
Alejandro Jodorowsky, 1967 93m

This film's one-page long screenplay follows Jodorowsky's drug-laced memories of a play by Fernando Arrabal. In an improvised production, a lost couple searches for the mythical city of Tar, and moves through a torpid landscape beset by a cast of absurd characters. The film is hypnagogic allegory, a topology of perverse desires, masochistic gestures, seduction and betrayal. The premiere of Fando y Lis in 1968 incited a riot, forcing the director to escape in a limousine. The film was immediately banned. It has been considered lost for much of the past three decades. May 31 7:00
Koneko Monogatari: The Adventures of Milo and Otis
Masanori Hata, 1986 90m

A kitten and dog are barnyard friends who often cause light-hearted mischief. When Milo (kitten) decides to explore the inside of a box and is swept away down the river, Otis (pug dog) is quick to leap into the water as well. Their river mishap opens the doors to an outdoor adventure in which they are separated and search for each other through valleys, mountains, snow and bears. Originally in Japanese, the English version is narrated by Dudley Moore. 35mm

Wednesdays - Hindi Hearthrobs: Khan Men and Others

March 30 7:00
Lagaan
Ashutosh Gowariker, 2001 224m

Mainstream America's favorite Bollywood movie (it even got an Oscar nomination!), Lagaan is in some ways atypical. It's set in the past, a sympathetic character is English, and there's no wedding. But it's still got the hallmarks of Hindi cinema: big songs and cricket. Bhuvan (Aamir Khan) makes a bargain with a wicked English official (Paul Blackthorne); if his villagers can win a cricket match against the English, they won't have to pay taxes. If they lose, the tax is harsher than ever. Do the underdog villagers have a shot? Well, they have Gauri (Gracy Singh) and Elizabeth (Rachel Shelley) on their side. Fall in love with Aamir as he sings and dances on the roof. Khan count: One. 35mm

April 6 7:00
Bollywood/Hollywood
Deepa Mehta, 2002 105m

Acclaimed filmmaker Deepa Mehta (Fire, Earth) mocks every Bollywood cliché there is in this Indian/Canadian version of Pretty Woman. There is a long-suffering widowed mother, a grandmother who has advice for every occasion, and a surprisingly heroic transvestite. Even the dance numbers are tongue-in-cheek. As his sister's wedding approaches, Rahul Khanna is forced to hire a call girl to act as his future wife. Bonus points for recognizing all Bollywood movies referenced in this film (at least 12). Fall in love with Rahul Khanna as he interviews potential brides and Akshaye Khanna as he dances at the "sing-song." Khanna count: 2. 35mm

April 13 7:00
The Hero
Anil Sharma, 2003 160m

Despite what you may think, Bollywood isn't only melodrama and romance. Well, okay, it is mostly melodrama and romance, but not completely. This action movie is a far cry from Bollywood's weepy soaps. Master spy Arjun Khanna (Sunny Deol) survives avalanches, leaps mountaintops, and pretends to be a nuclear scientist in order to prevent Kashmiri insurgents from assembling a bomb in Canada (it's complicated). He also takes time to woo a shepherdess (Preity Zinta) and a doctor (Priyanka Chopra). Plus, there are musical numbers. Top that, James Bond! Fall in love with Sunny Deol as he saves the day again and again. Khan count: Zero. 35mm

Sooraj R. Barjatya, 1994 206m

Nisha (Madhuri Dixit) and Prem (Salman Khan) meet when Nisha's older sister and Prem's older brother get engaged. It seems that fate enduces the two hours of flirting that follow. After this, the plot kicks into gear. This results in an hour of tears and family sacrifice that are not to be missed. A heart-warming favorite that ran for years in theaters (and is now out on special-edition DVD with karaoke), the film has all the Bollywood essentials. There are fourteen songs, Madhuri Dixit dances up a storm, and Salman Khan takes his shirt off. Salman, when he sings "Pehla Pehla Pyaar" in the kitchen, is not to be missed. Khan count: One. 35mm

Rohit (Hrithik Roshan) isn't quite healthy. His physical weakness and childish behavior can doubtless be traced back to the car crash that his mother was in while pregnant. Why did the car crash? Because of the flying saucer summoned by Rohit's father, of course! Now Rohit's grown up and, with Nisha's (Preity Zinta) help, he summons the aliens just like his father did. Mohabbatein

Narayan Shankar (Amitabh Bachchan) has been in charge of Gurukul forever. As long as he's been in charge, there's been a strict rule against student romance. Of course, our three heroes (Uday Chopra, Jugal Hansraj, and Jimmy Shergill) fall in love anyway. May 18 7:00
Kabhi Khushi Kabhie Gham
Karan Johar, 2001 210m

"It's all about loving your parents." And, of course, adoption issues, love across class boundaries, and fitting three hot couples in one movie. Rahul (Shah Rukh Khan) marries Anjali (Kajol) against his father's wishes and skedaddles to London. Two hours of film later, he's reunited with his family with the help of his brother Rohan (Hrithik Roshan) and sister Poo (Kareena Kapoor). But it really is all about the parents: played by real-life husband and wife Amitabh and Jaya Bachchan, they steal every scene they're in. It's just like When Harry Met Sally. Except that Sally is Rhea (Rani Mukherjee), and Harry is Karan (Saif Ali Khan), and there are musical numbers, and they didn't go to U of C. Okay, so it's not just like When Harry Met Sally. But it is a remake of it, and it is a lot of fun. Rhea and Karan fight during a layover in Amsterdam, start to like each other at Rhea's wedding, make friends in Paris, and then in Mumbai. Highlights include Rishi Kapoor as Karan's father, Absishek Bachchan as the most attractive computer engineer ever, and Karan's surprisingly effective use of pink pom-poms. Saif has a defense of the "Tom Cruise look" not to be missed. Khan count: One. 35mm

The Bollywood tearjerker to end all Bollywood tearjerkers (and it's set in New York!). Naina (Preity Zinta) and Rohit (Saif Ali Khan) are platonic friends when Aman (Shah Rukh Khan) shows up. Soon Naina falls for Aman, Rohit falls for Naina, and for a minute it looks like Aman falls for Rohit. It eventually gets straightened out, but not before hospital scenes, songs, and dramatic speeches. This film also includes Jaya Bhaduri (in a great performance as Naina's mother), a surprisingly practical six-day plan to get a girl, and the hands-down best-ever version of "Pretty Woman." Saif charms yet again when he tries to walk cool. Khan count: Two. 35mm

Young Charlie Bucket yearns to be like his fellow classmates. Unfortunately, due to his family's extreme poverty, he is forced to work a paper route instead. Willy Wonka, a creative genius, eccentric chocolateer, and controller of an international confection industry, holds a competition that allows the finders of 5 golden tickets to visit his mysterious factory. Will Charlie realize his dream even with the competition of so many other children from around the world? 35mm

Robert Zemeckis, 1988 103 m

In a world where toons and humans coexist, Roger Rabbit is afraid that his wife, the lovely and sensuous Jessica Rabbit, may be seeing someone else. After many adventures, Mrs. Brisby ends up at the home of a highly intelligent rat collective under the farmer's rose bush. Led by the wise Nicodemos, it seems that the rats are going to help Mrs. Brisby save her son and relocate; but why are they helping her and why are they so highly intelligent? 35mm
